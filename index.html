The Soma

Haymarket, New South Wales, Australia

Designed by Ruben Sivarajah and Stephan Hoang

x

The Problems

In Sydney the majority of people waste around 2 hours per day just on travelling to work and other locations, time which could be better spent towards bettering our society. Furthermore, current methods of transport can be dangerous and harmful to the environment.

The Idea

The Plexus Infinity is modelled off the neural network of the human brain. As each neuron branches off toward other neurons at the speed of light so too will our hyperloop stations refered to as ‘Soma’, with the connections between stations 'Axons', station will have at least 3 Axons leading out to other nearby stations. The pods, ‘Pulses’, will continually move at up to 1120 kmh (700 mph) only slowing down as they loop around a Soma to collect passengers then regain speed as they continue on to the next station.

The Technology

Utilising Electromagnetic Suspension technology as used by Maglev trains in Japan, Pulses will implement a surrounding electromagnetic field to prevent speed loss from any friction as it levitates within low pressure tube. Each Axon will be covered with a ‘solar panel sleeve’ to help power the Plexus Infinity.
